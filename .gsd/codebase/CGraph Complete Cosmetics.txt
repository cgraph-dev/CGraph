The problem is zero unlock conditions on any cosmetic — the cosmetics API endpoints (/api/v1/cosmetics/borders, /api/v1/cosmetics/titles, /api/v1/cosmetics/badges) all return [] as TODOs. You have a fully built cosmetic wardrobe with no door. This document gives every cosmetic a door.
​

The MOBA/Gacha Reference Model
Games like League of Legends and Genshin Impact have perfected cosmetic reward systems over a decade of iteration. The principles that make them work:

LoL Honor System: 6 levels of behavioral reputation, each with capsules and orbs gated behind consistent positive play — cosmetics are proof of social standing, not just time investment.
​

Genshin Namecards: Unlocked via completing entire Achievement categories, reaching Friendship Level 10 with characters, Reputation levels per nation, Battle Pass, and limited events — each namecard type signals what kind of player you are.
​

The core insight: "Cosmetic incentives such as namecards motivate players to try harder in events" because the reward has social signal value even though it has zero gameplay impact.
​

Scarcity principle: The most prized cosmetic rewards are the ones tied to activities that most players will never complete — scarcity creates prestige.
​

CGraph is both a messaging app (like Discord) and a community builder (like Reddit + phpBB). This means your user archetypes are far broader than a MOBA: you have chatters, forum contributors, group admins, content creators, social butterflies, security-conscious users, moderation contributors, and power users. Every archetype deserves a cosmetic path.

Milestone Trigger Architecture
Every unlock condition above maps to a domain event in your existing backend contexts.
​

text
CGraph.Messaging    → message_sent, secret_chat_session, e2ee_key_verified
CGraph.Forums       → post_created, helpful_vote_received, forum_created
CGraph.Groups       → group_joined, group_created, member_count_threshold
CGraph.Accounts     → account_age_milestone, security_feature_enabled
CGraph.Nodes        → tip_sent, nodes_earned_milestone, nodes_spent_milestone
CGraph.Social       → friend_added, referral_converted
Each event fires CGraph.Cosmetics.Unlock.check_and_grant/2 — evaluates the user against all unlock conditions for that event type, grants any newly eligible cosmetics, and pushes a user:cosmetic_unlocked notification via UserChannel. This is intentionally lightweight — no XP system, no quest log, no level bar. Just silent milestone checking triggered by real activity, exactly how LoL's honor system rewards behavior without making it the center of attention.
